# Chart Explanations

## C01 Industry Risk Dimensions Heatmap
The heatmap shows how public-data-derived classification dimensions accumulate into the industry view. Across the portfolio, external shock is the most severe average dimension, while Health Care and Social Assistance carries the highest base score at 3.32.

## C02 Industry Base Risk Score by Sector
This ranking is the core sector risk view for credit analysis. Health Care and Social Assistance is currently the highest-risk sector at 3.32, while Transport, Postal And Warehousing is lowest at 1.95. These scores combine public ABS/RBA signals with public-data-derived classification scores.

## C03 Employment Growth by Industry
Employment growth is one of the cleanest public signals for near-term sector momentum. Professional, Scientific And Technical Services shows the strongest YoY growth at +5.5%, while Wholesale Trade is weakest at -8.7%.

## C04 Borrower Industry Risk Scorecard
This scorecard uses workbook hard-coded borrower archetype financial statements because borrower-level accounts are not public. Health Care & Social Assistance Archetype is the highest-risk archetype at 2.97, while Transport, Postal And Warehousing Archetype is lowest at 2.09.

## C05 Indicative Pricing by Borrower
Indicative pricing is driven by illustrative workbook pricing settings layered onto borrower risk scores. All borrower archetypes currently price at a uniform 6.60% all-in rate under the hard-coded pricing assumptions.

## C06 Sector Concentration: Current Exposure vs Limit
This chart compares the workbook portfolio exposure proxy against illustrative concentration limits. The highest utilisation is Retail Trade at 85.0% of limit. No sector currently breaches the hard-coded concentration limits.

## C07 Industry Watchlist Trigger Count
Watchlist counts translate public ABS/RBA warning signals into a monitoring queue. Agriculture, Forestry And Fishing has the highest number of triggers at 4, which should drive intensified sector review and obligor screening.

## C08 Industry Stress Test Impact
The stress matrix applies simplified APRA-informed scenario shocks to the public-data sector view. Demand shock produces the largest average uplift at 0.23 score points, and Health Care and Social Assistance reaches the highest stressed score at 3.55.
